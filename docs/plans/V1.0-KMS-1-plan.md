# V1.0-KMS-1 — KMS Production Readiness — Implementation Plan

Status: Draft
Owner: Crypto
Priority: P0
Labels: `milestone:v1.0`, `area:kms`, `area:crypto`, `area:observability`

> Scope clarified relative to the tracker entry at
> `docs/issues/v1.0-issues.md:22`. The issue is a high-level P0 hardening
> brief. This plan expands it into a full specification covering:
> connection pooling and exponential-backoff retry wrappers for network-backed
> KMS adapters; a DEK-caching `KeyManager` decorator with configurable TTL and
> eviction metrics; a periodic health-check background goroutine that drives the
> existing `gateway_kms_healthy` Prometheus gauge; circuit-breaker semantics
> (fail-closed after configurable thresholds); health-check integration for the
> AWS KMS and Vault Transit adapters (both are planned but unimplemented at the
> time of planning); and a KMS outage runbook section with degraded-mode
> documentation.

## 1. Context & Current State

### 1.1 KeyManager interface and existing adapters

The `KeyManager` interface lives at `internal/crypto/keymanager.go:24`. It
exposes six methods: `Provider()`, `WrapKey()`, `UnwrapKey()`,
`ActiveKeyVersion()`, `HealthCheck()`, and `Close()`.

Three network-backed production adapters exist today:

- `cosmianKMIPManager` (`internal/crypto/keymanager_cosmian.go:41`) — connects
  to an external Cosmian KMIP server. It carries a single `*kmipclient.Client`
  field with no pooling and a fixed 5 s timeout (`keymanager_cosmian.go:89`).
  `HealthCheck` does a lightweight KMIP `Get` on the active key
  (`keymanager_cosmian.go:240–262`).

- `cosmianKMIPJSONManager` (`internal/crypto/keymanager_cosmian_json.go`) — the
  HTTP/JSON variant of the same adapter. Its `HealthCheck` follows the same
  pattern.

- `inMemoryKeyManager` (`internal/crypto/keymanager_memory.go:49`) — local only;
  no network I/O.

The `self_contained` adapters (`keymanager_selfcontained_aes.go`,
`keymanager_selfcontained_rsa.go`) are local-only; their `HealthCheck` is a
struct-field check, not an I/O operation.

The AWS KMS adapter (`internal/crypto/keymanager_aws.go`) and the HashiCorp
Vault Transit adapter (`internal/crypto/keymanager_vault.go`) are referenced in
the issue tracker (`docs/issues/v1.0-issues.md:52,90`) but **not yet
implemented**. V1.0-KMS-1 must prepare the HA harness that both adapters will
plug into; the adapters themselves are tracked by V1.0-KMS-2 and V1.0-KMS-3.

### 1.2 DEK wrap/unwrap call sites

`internal/crypto/engine.go` calls `WrapKey` and `UnwrapKey` on the injected
`KeyManager` at:
- `engine.go:513` (chunked encrypt path — WrapKey)
- `engine.go:766` (chunked decrypt path — UnwrapKey)
- `engine.go:1024` (MPU encrypt — WrapKey)
- `engine.go:1156` (MPU finalize — WrapKey)
- `engine.go:1344,1354` (MPU decrypt — UnwrapKey)
- `engine.go:1543,1553` (range-decrypt fallback — UnwrapKey)

Every call goes synchronously to the KMS with no caching, no retry, and no
circuit-breaking. A KMS hiccup lasting even a few hundred milliseconds can
cascade into elevated encryption-error rates.

### 1.3 Health-check wiring

`internal/metrics/metrics.go:110` defines the `kmsHealthy *prometheus.GaugeVec`
gauge, initialized at `metrics.go:492`. The helper `SetKMSHealthy(provider, bool)`
exists at `metrics.go:991`. However, `cmd/server/main.go` never calls
`SetKMSHealthy` — there is no periodic goroutine invoking `HealthCheck` on the
active `KeyManager` and updating this gauge. The `gateway_kms_healthy` metric
therefore stays at its zero-value after startup, making the alert rule
`S3GatewayKMSUnhealthy` (`gateway_kms_healthy == 0`) fire spuriously on every
deploy even when the KMS is healthy.

### 1.4 Circuit-breaker and retry gaps

`internal/api/crypto_factory.go:44` (BuildKeyManager) constructs a bare
`KeyManager` from config. There is no decorator layer implementing backoff or
circuit-breaking between the engine and the raw KMS adapter. The S3 backend
retry policy (`internal/api/crypto_factory.go` and `V0.6-PERF-2`) covers only
the S3 object-store path; it does not touch the KMS path.

`github.com/cenkalti/backoff/v4` and `/v5` are already in `go.mod:58–59`,
providing a ready-to-use exponential-backoff implementation.

### 1.5 Why the current state is insufficient

Three production failure scenarios are unhandled today:

1. **Transient KMS timeout** — a single `WrapKey` or `UnwrapKey` call fails with
   a network timeout. The engine immediately returns an error to the S3 client;
   no retry is attempted.

2. **KMS unavailability cascade** — every S3 operation that touches an encrypted
   object drives a new KMS round-trip. If the KMS is down for 30 s, every
   concurrent request fails. A circuit breaker would absorb the load and fail
   fast with a clear error rather than letting every goroutine block until
   timeout.

3. **Invisible health state** — the `gateway_kms_healthy` gauge stays at 0
   permanently (never set to 1). Operators cannot reliably alert on actual KMS
   outages.

## 2. Design Goals & Non-Goals

### Goals

1. **Retry-with-backoff decorator** — a `RetryingKeyManager` wrapper retries
   transient KMS errors (network I/O failures, timeouts) with configurable
   exponential backoff and jitter using the existing `cenkalti/backoff` library.
   Permanent errors (`ErrUnwrapFailed`, `ErrInvalidEnvelope`) are never retried.

2. **Circuit-breaker decorator** — a `CircuitBreakerKeyManager` wrapper
   implements the three-state (Closed / Open / Half-Open) circuit-breaker
   pattern. When open, `WrapKey` and `UnwrapKey` fail immediately with
   `ErrProviderUnavailable`; the engine can then signal a 503 to clients rather
   than blocking until timeout.

3. **DEK-wrap/unwrap cache** — a `CachingKeyManager` decorator caches
   `UnwrapKey` results keyed by `KeyEnvelope.Ciphertext` for re-reads of the
   same object within a configurable TTL (default 60 s). Cache eviction and
   hit/miss are exposed as Prometheus counters. Cache entries are zeroized on
   eviction and on `Close()`.

4. **Periodic health-check goroutine** — started in `cmd/server/main.go`,
   calls `KeyManager.HealthCheck` every `health_check_interval` (default 30 s)
   and updates `gateway_kms_healthy` via `metrics.SetKMSHealthy`. Eliminates
   the spurious zero-gauge problem.

5. **Health-check endpoint for AWS KMS and Vault Transit** — `HealthCheck`
   contracts are defined here so that V1.0-KMS-2 and V1.0-KMS-3 can implement
   them against these specs. Stub implementations return `ErrProviderUnavailable`
   until the adapters are built.

6. **Configuration** — all new decorators are opt-in and configurable via
   `KeyManagerConfig` sub-structs; default values enable the retry and
   health-check goroutine but leave the circuit breaker and DEK cache off until
   explicitly enabled.

7. **KMS outage runbook** — `docs/RUNBOOK.md` is extended with a dedicated
   "KMS outage — degraded mode" section describing operator actions and the
   fail-closed guarantee.

8. **Observability** — four new Prometheus metrics (cache hits, cache misses,
   circuit-breaker state, retry attempt count) are added.

### Non-Goals

1. **AWS KMS adapter implementation** — tracked by V1.0-KMS-2. This plan only
   specifies the `HealthCheck` contract that V1.0-KMS-2 must satisfy.

2. **Vault Transit adapter implementation** — tracked by V1.0-KMS-3; same caveat.

3. **WrapKey result caching** — wrapping is called once per object write and the
   result is persisted in object metadata; caching wrap results provides minimal
   throughput gain and complicates zeroization. Only `UnwrapKey` results are
   cached (the hot read path).

4. **Persistent DEK cache across restarts** — the cache is strictly in-process
   and in-memory; no file or Redis persistence. Objects persisted across restarts
   will pay the KMS round-trip on first access after restart.

5. **Multi-level cache (L1 + L2)** — a single in-memory LRU is sufficient for
   the expected object access patterns; a Redis-backed L2 is deferred.

## 3. Target Interface / Semantics / Behaviour

### 3.1 `RetryingKeyManager` decorator

```go
// RetryingKeyManager wraps a KeyManager and retries transient errors using
// exponential backoff with full jitter.
//
// Invariants:
//   - Thread-safe: delegates to the inner KeyManager; no internal state mutation.
//   - Only WrapKey and UnwrapKey are retried; HealthCheck is not.
//   - Permanent errors (ErrUnwrapFailed, ErrInvalidEnvelope, ErrKeyNotFound,
//     ErrProviderUnavailable) are never retried; the first error is returned
//     immediately.
//   - ctx cancellation terminates the retry loop immediately (backoff.Retry
//     checks ctx.Done on each iteration).
//   - MaxElapsedTime caps the total time across all attempts; 0 means no cap.
//   - If all retries are exhausted, the last error is returned wrapped with
//     fmt.Errorf("keymanager/retry: max elapsed time exceeded: %w", lastErr).
type RetryingKeyManager struct {
    inner          KeyManager
    cfg            RetryConfig
}

// RetryConfig holds exponential-backoff parameters for KMS retries.
type RetryConfig struct {
    // InitialInterval is the wait before the first retry. Default: 100 ms.
    InitialInterval time.Duration
    // MaxInterval caps the per-attempt delay. Default: 5 s.
    MaxInterval time.Duration
    // MaxElapsedTime caps the total retry window. Default: 30 s.
    // Set to 0 to retry indefinitely (ctx cancellation is the only stop).
    MaxElapsedTime time.Duration
    // Multiplier is the backoff multiplier between attempts. Default: 2.0.
    Multiplier float64
}
```

**Retry policy:** Only errors NOT matching `errors.Is(err, ErrUnwrapFailed) ||
errors.Is(err, ErrInvalidEnvelope) || errors.Is(err, ErrKeyNotFound) ||
errors.Is(err, ErrProviderUnavailable)` are retried. These sentinel errors
indicate permanent rejection by the KMS, not transient network issues.

**Rationale:** The `cenkalti/backoff` package already in `go.mod` provides
`backoff.WithContext` and `backoff.ExponentialBackOff` which precisely match
this use case. Using it avoids a custom retry-state-machine and leverages the
project's existing dep.

### 3.2 `CircuitBreakerKeyManager` decorator

```go
// CircuitBreakerKeyManager wraps a KeyManager with a three-state circuit breaker.
//
// Invariants:
//   - Thread-safe: all state transitions are guarded by a sync.Mutex.
//   - States: Closed (normal), Open (failing fast), Half-Open (probe).
//   - WrapKey and UnwrapKey are circuit-guarded; HealthCheck bypasses the
//     circuit so the health-check goroutine always reaches the KMS.
//   - When Open, WrapKey/UnwrapKey return ErrProviderUnavailable immediately,
//     without calling the inner KeyManager.
//   - After OpenTimeout elapses, one probe attempt is allowed (Half-Open).
//     On success → Closed; on failure → Open with reset timer.
//   - Failure threshold: ConsecutiveFailures consecutive errors trip the breaker.
//   - SuccessThreshold: consecutive successes in Half-Open to close the breaker.
type CircuitBreakerKeyManager struct {
    inner  KeyManager
    cfg    CircuitBreakerConfig
    mu     sync.Mutex
    state  cbState            // closed, open, halfOpen
    failures   int
    successes  int
    openedAt   time.Time
}

type CircuitBreakerConfig struct {
    // ConsecutiveFailures trips the breaker after N consecutive failures.
    // Default: 5.
    ConsecutiveFailures int
    // OpenTimeout is how long the breaker stays open before probing.
    // Default: 30 s.
    OpenTimeout time.Duration
    // SuccessThreshold closes the breaker after N consecutive probe successes.
    // Default: 2.
    SuccessThreshold int
}
```

**State-transition table:**

| From | Event | To |
|------|-------|-----|
| Closed | N consecutive failures | Open (record `openedAt`) |
| Closed | success | Closed (reset failure counter) |
| Open | `openedAt + OpenTimeout` not elapsed | Open (return ErrProviderUnavailable) |
| Open | `openedAt + OpenTimeout` elapsed | Half-Open (allow one call) |
| Half-Open | success | +1 `successes`; if successes ≥ SuccessThreshold → Closed |
| Half-Open | failure | Open (reset `openedAt`) |

**Rationale:** A minimal circuit breaker without a third-party dependency is
preferable: the state machine is < 80 lines, is easily unit-tested, and avoids
importing `sony/gobreaker` or similar. The `mu sync.Mutex` guarantee ensures
that the failure counter and state field are never torn.

### 3.3 `CachingKeyManager` decorator

```go
// CachingKeyManager wraps a KeyManager and caches UnwrapKey results in a
// fixed-size LRU (Least-Recently-Used) map.
//
// Invariants:
//   - Thread-safe: all cache operations are guarded by a sync.RWMutex.
//   - Cache key: SHA-256 of envelope.Ciphertext (32-byte fingerprint, never
//     stores plaintext key material as the map key).
//   - Cache value: plaintext DEK bytes + expiry time.Time.
//   - On Get hit: a copy of the cached DEK is returned; the caller owns the copy
//     and is responsible for zeroization.
//   - On eviction (TTL expiry or capacity): the cached DEK bytes are zeroized.
//   - Close: all cached DEK bytes are zeroized before the cache is cleared.
//   - WrapKey is never cached (see Non-Goals §2.3).
//   - Expired entries are lazily evicted on access or via a background cleanup
//     ticker (cleanupInterval default: TTL/2).
type CachingKeyManager struct {
    inner          KeyManager
    cfg            CacheConfig
    mu             sync.RWMutex
    entries        map[[32]byte]*cacheEntry   // fingerprint → entry
    lruOrder       []([32]byte)               // insertion-ordered for LRU eviction
}

type cacheEntry struct {
    plaintext []byte
    expiresAt time.Time
}

// DEKCacheConfig holds configuration for the CachingKeyManager.
type DEKCacheConfig struct {
    // Enabled enables the DEK unwrap cache. Default: false.
    Enabled bool `yaml:"enabled" env:"KMS_DEK_CACHE_ENABLED"`
    // TTL is the duration a cached DEK unwrap result is valid.
    // Default: 60 s.
    TTL time.Duration `yaml:"ttl" env:"KMS_DEK_CACHE_TTL"`
    // MaxEntries is the maximum number of cached entries (LRU eviction).
    // Default: 1000.
    MaxEntries int `yaml:"max_entries" env:"KMS_DEK_CACHE_MAX_ENTRIES"`
    // CleanupInterval is how often the background cleanup ticker runs.
    // Default: TTL/2, minimum 5 s.
    CleanupInterval time.Duration `yaml:"cleanup_interval" env:"KMS_DEK_CACHE_CLEANUP_INTERVAL"`
}
```

**Cache key design:** SHA-256 of `envelope.Ciphertext` is used as the map key.
This is cryptographically sound: two different plaintexts produce different
ciphertexts (AEAD nonce uniqueness); SHA-256 fingerprints are fixed-size and
safe to use as map keys without exposing plaintext.

**Zeroization on eviction:** when an entry is evicted (TTL, LRU, or `Close`),
`zeroBytes(entry.plaintext)` is called before `delete(m.entries, key)`.

**Rationale:** The read hot path in the gateway is `GetObject` which calls
`UnwrapKey` once per object fetch. Re-reading the same object repeatedly (e.g.
a video being replayed) pays a KMS round-trip on every request without caching.
A 60 s TTL covers typical HTTP byte-range resume patterns. The SHA-256 key
ensures no DEK is recoverable from the cache map keys alone.

### 3.7 HealthCheck contract for AWS KMS and Vault Transit (stub spec)

Per V1.0-KMS-2 and V1.0-KMS-3, both adapters MUST implement `HealthCheck` as follows:

**AWS KMS adapter** (`internal/crypto/keymanager_aws.go`):
- Call `kms.DescribeKey` on the configured key ARN or alias.
- Honour `ctx` cancellation.
- Return `nil` if the response indicates the key is `ENABLED`.
- Return `fmt.Errorf("aws-kms: health check: key state %s: %w", state, ErrProviderUnavailable)` for any other state.

**Vault Transit adapter** (`internal/crypto/keymanager_vault.go`):
- Call `GET /v1/<transit_mount>/keys/<key_name>`.
- Honour `ctx` cancellation.
- Return `nil` if the key exists and `deletion_allowed` is `false` (key is active).
- Return `fmt.Errorf("vault-transit: health check: %w", err)` on any API error.

Both adapters MUST NOT perform an encrypt/decrypt operation for the health check (read-only verification only).

### Phase C — `RetryingKeyManager` implementation

**C1.** `internal/crypto/keymanager_retry.go` — **new**

Implement `RetryingKeyManager`:

```go
// NewRetryingKeyManager wraps inner with exponential-backoff retry on
// transient KMS errors. Only WrapKey and UnwrapKey are retried.
// Provide a nil metrics to disable metric recording.
func NewRetryingKeyManager(inner KeyManager, cfg RetryConfig, m *metrics.Metrics) KeyManager
```

Code sketch for `WrapKey`:
```go
func (r *RetryingKeyManager) WrapKey(ctx context.Context, plaintext []byte, meta map[string]string) (*KeyEnvelope, error) {
    var result *KeyEnvelope
    op := func() error {
        env, err := r.inner.WrapKey(ctx, plaintext, meta)
        if err != nil {
            if isPermanentKMSError(err) {
                return backoff.Permanent(err)
            }
            if r.metrics != nil {
                r.metrics.RecordKMSRetryAttempt(r.inner.Provider(), "wrap", "failure")
            }
            return err
        }
        result = env
        if r.metrics != nil {
            r.metrics.RecordKMSRetryAttempt(r.inner.Provider(), "wrap", "success")
        }
        return nil
    }
    bo := r.newBackOff(ctx)
    if err := backoff.Retry(op, bo); err != nil {
        return nil, fmt.Errorf("keymanager/retry: %w", err)
    }
    return result, nil
}
```

`isPermanentKMSError(err error) bool` returns true if `errors.Is(err,
ErrUnwrapFailed) || errors.Is(err, ErrInvalidEnvelope) || errors.Is(err,

### Phase E — `CachingKeyManager` implementation

**E1.** `internal/crypto/keymanager_cache.go` — **new**

Implement `CachingKeyManager` per §3.3.

```go
func NewCachingKeyManager(inner KeyManager, cfg DEKCacheConfig, m *metrics.Metrics) (KeyManager, error)
```

`UnwrapKey` logic:
1. Compute `fingerprint = sha256.Sum256(envelope.Ciphertext)`.
2. RLock; check `entries[fingerprint]`; if found and not expired: copy plaintext,
   increment cache-hit counter, return copy.
3. Unlock; call `inner.UnwrapKey`.
4. Lock; store new entry (copy of plaintext + `time.Now().Add(cfg.TTL)`); enforce
   LRU eviction if `len(entries) > cfg.MaxEntries`; increment cache-miss counter.

LRU eviction: evict the entry at `lruOrder[0]`, calling `zeroBytes(entry.plaintext)`
before deletion. The `lruOrder` slice is appended on insert and the oldest entry
is index 0.

Background cleanup ticker: a goroutine started in the constructor iterates the
map every `cleanupInterval`, zeroizing and deleting entries where `time.Now().After(entry.expiresAt)`.
The goroutine exits when `Close()` is called (via a `stopCleanup chan struct{}`).

`Close` stops the cleanup goroutine, zeroizes all cached plaintexts, delegates
to `inner.Close()`.

Compile-time assertion: `var _ KeyManager = (*CachingKeyManager)(nil)`.

**G1.** `internal/crypto/keymanager_healthcheck.go` — **new**

```go
// StartKMSHealthCheck starts a background goroutine that calls
// km.HealthCheck every interval and updates the Prometheus gauge via m.

```

Implementation:
```go
func StartKMSHealthCheck(ctx context.Context, km KeyManager, interval time.Duration, m *metrics.Metrics) func() {
    if interval == 0 {
        return func() {}
    }
    stopCh := make(chan struct{})
    go func() {
        ticker := time.NewTicker(interval)
        defer ticker.Stop()
        for {
            select {
            case <-ticker.C:
                hctx, cancel := context.WithTimeout(ctx, interval/2)
                err := km.HealthCheck(hctx)
                cancel()
                m.SetKMSHealthy(km.Provider(), err == nil)
            case <-stopCh:
                return
            case <-ctx.Done():
                return
            }
        }
    }()
    return func() { close(stopCh) }
}
```

The health-check context timeout is `interval/2` to ensure the check always
completes before the next tick. An initial health check is fired immediately
on goroutine start (before the first tick) to populate the gauge at startup.

```
### kms-outage-degraded-mode

**Scenario:** KMS is fully unavailable (network partition, KMS service down).

**Behaviour under V1.0-KMS-1 harness:**
- The retry wrapper will exhaust `max_elapsed_time` (default 30 s) per request,
  then return an error.
- If the circuit breaker is enabled (recommended), after `consecutive_failures`
  failures (default 5) it trips open and all subsequent WrapKey/UnwrapKey calls
  fail immediately with `ErrProviderUnavailable` (503 to clients). This prevents
  goroutine pile-up during prolonged outages.
- The DEK cache (if enabled) continues to serve previously-cached unwrap results
  for up to `ttl` seconds (default 60 s). READ operations for recently-accessed
  objects continue to succeed from cache; write operations (new PutObject,
  UploadPart) fail immediately.
- The health-check goroutine updates `gateway_kms_healthy` to 0, triggering the
  `S3GatewayKMSUnhealthy` alert.

**Recovery:**
1. When the KMS recovers, the circuit breaker Half-Open probe succeeds and the
   breaker closes automatically.
2. The health-check goroutine updates `gateway_kms_healthy` to 1.
3. No gateway restart is required.

**Fail-closed guarantee:** Write operations (new object encryption) always
require a successful `WrapKey` call; the DEK cache covers only reads. A
gateway running with a downed KMS will accept GET requests for cached objects
but reject PUT/POST operations. This is the correct degraded-mode posture.
```

## 7. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| DEK cache returns stale plaintext after key rotation (new active key version wraps new DEKs; cache still holds old-version plaintexts) | Cache key is the envelope `Ciphertext` bytes (per-object); old-version unwrapped plaintexts remain valid for the objects they were used to decrypt — this is correct. Rotation changes which key wraps NEW objects, not old ones. No cross-version stale risk. |
| SHA-256 collision in cache key allows one object's DEK to satisfy another object's cache lookup | SHA-256 collision resistance renders this computationally infeasible. Add an explicit test asserting distinct ciphertexts produce distinct fingerprints. |
| `zeroBytes(plaintext)` called on the cached copy but not the caller's copy | The cache stores a private copy; `UnwrapKey` returns a second copy owned by the caller. Only the cache's copy is zeroized on eviction; the caller must zeroize their copy per `KeyManager` invariant 3. Documented clearly in code comment and test. |
| Circuit breaker in Open state during planned KMS maintenance window causes all write operations to fail | Document in the runbook: pre-disable the circuit breaker (`circuit_breaker.enabled: false`) before planned KMS maintenance. Or: increase `open_timeout` to exceed the maintenance window. |
| Background cleanup goroutine in `CachingKeyManager` leaks if `Close` is never called | Cleanup goroutine listens on `stopCleanup chan struct{}`; a finalizer-based warning (log + counter increment) is added if the cache is GC'd without `Close`. Promoted to DoD. |
| `RetryingKeyManager` retries obscure the original error for operators | Last error is wrapped with `"keymanager/retry: max elapsed time exceeded: %w"` preserving the cause via `%w`. Log at `WARN` level on each retry attempt with attempt count and elapsed time. |
| `BuildKeyManager` now has a `*metrics.Metrics` parameter, breaking existing callers | There is exactly one production call site (`cmd/server/main.go`). The function is internal (package `api`, not exported from the module). Test callers in `internal/api/crypto_factory_test.go` pass `nil` for metrics. |
| Config validation for new fields may reject valid existing configs | All new fields have zero-value defaults that are valid (retry enabled with conservative defaults; circuit breaker disabled; cache disabled). Old configs that do not mention the new fields are unaffected. |

## 12. O'Reilly Verification Gaps (2026-06-08)

> Added by plan-verifier after cross-referencing with O'Reilly authoritative sources.
> Status as of 2026-06-09: all gaps resolved.

### GAP-KMS1-1 — ✅ RESOLVED — HIGH — CachingKeyManager LRU is actually FIFO

**Source:** *Concurrency in Go* (Cox-Buday, O'Reilly, 2017), Ch. 6 — goroutine-safe data structures; correct LRU requires O(1) hit-time reordering.

**Description:** The plan describes `lruOrder []([32]byte)` as "insertion-ordered for LRU eviction" and evicts `lruOrder[0]`. A real LRU must move a fingerprint to the tail on every cache **hit** so that the least-recently-**used** entry is at the head. The plan never mentions updating `lruOrder` on a cache hit — only on insert. This implements FIFO, not LRU. Under a skewed access pattern where one hot object is read constantly but was inserted early, it will be evicted while cold entries inserted later survive.

**Required fix:** Replace `lruOrder []([32]byte)` with `*container/list.List` for the LRU order and a parallel `map[[32]byte]*list.Element` for O(1) lookup-and-promote. On every cache hit (inside the RLock section, promoted to a full Lock), call `lruList.MoveToBack(element)`. On eviction, call `lruList.Front()`. Document the O(1) hit-time cost in the struct comment.

### GAP-KMS1-2 — ✅ RESOLVED — HIGH — Double-close panic on `stopCleanup chan struct{}`

**Source:** Go specification — calling `close()` on an already-closed channel panics.

**Description:** Phase E1 specifies `Close()` calls `close(stopCleanup)`. If `Close()` is called concurrently or twice, this panics. The `KeyManager` invariant requires idempotent `Close` (§3.1). The plan's Risks section only addresses "Close never called" — not "Close called twice".

**Required fix:** Guard `close(stopCleanup)` with a `sync.Once` inside `CachingKeyManager`. The `once` in `Close()` ensures second calls are no-ops. Also set the `closed bool` guard before closing the channel.

### GAP-KMS1-3 — ✅ RESOLVED — MEDIUM — Successful HealthCheck cannot close an open circuit breaker

**Source:** *Release It! 2nd Ed.* (Nygard, Pragmatic 2018), Ch. 5 "Stability Patterns" — circuit breaker recovery paths.

**Description:** The plan correctly specifies that `HealthCheck` bypasses the circuit breaker. However, nowhere does the plan specify that a successful HealthCheck result drives the CB from Open back toward Half-Open or Closed. During an extended KMS outage followed by recovery, the gateway will not begin serving again until a WrapKey/UnwrapKey call is made — the periodic health-check goroutine detecting a healthy KMS does nothing to close the breaker. This extends the outage blast radius.

**Required fix:** Define a deliberate policy for HealthCheck-driven CB recovery in §3.2 and the runbook. Options: (a) expose `ResetIfHealthy(ctx)` on `CircuitBreakerKeyManager`, called from the health-check goroutine after `openedAt+OpenTimeout` elapses; or (b) explicitly document that HealthCheck recovery does NOT close the breaker and operators must rely on organic traffic. Either is valid but the plan must be explicit.
